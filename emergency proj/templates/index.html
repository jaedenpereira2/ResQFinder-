Basic Emergency Assistance

Learn essential skills to assist in critical emergency situations. These life-saving techniques can make a significant difference while waiting for professional medical help to arrive.

  • Performing CPR

    CPR can save lives by maintaining blood flow to the brain and heart until medical help arrives.
    Steps to Take During Sudden Cardiac Arrest
    1. Call for Help – Dial 102 immediately.
    2. Start CPR – Perform chest compressions, even if only hands-only CPR.
    3. Use an AED (if available – Follow the device instructions to deliver a shock.
    4. Continue CPR– Maintain chest compressions until medical help arrives.
    5. Defibrillation by Emergency Personnel – If no AED was used, paramedics will use a defibrillator.
    6. Medication Administration– Antiarrhythmic drugs may be given through an IV to stabilize heart rhythm. br 7. Hospital Care– Further treatment is needed to recover and prevent future cardiac issues.

  • Handling severe bleeding

    Steps to Handle Severe Bleeding Call for Help
    1. Dial 102 immediately. Apply Direct Pressure
    2. Use a clean cloth or bandage to press firmly on the wound. Elevate the Wounded Area
    3. If possible, raise the injury above heart level to slow bleeding. Use a Tourniquet (if necessary)
    4. If bleeding is uncontrollable, apply a tourniquet above the wound.Maintain Pressure
    5. Keep applying firm pressure until emergency help arrives. Monitor for Shock
    6. Look for signs like pale skin, rapid breathing, or confusion and keep the person warm.

  • Dealing with accidents

    Steps to Deal with Accidents Assess the Situation
    1. Ensure your safety before approaching the injured person. Call for Help
    2. Dial 102 or emergency services immediately.
    3. Check for Responsiveness – If unconscious, check breathing and pulse.
    4. Provide Basic First Aid
    - Severe bleeding – Apply direct pressure and elevate the wound.
    - Fractures – Immobilize the area and avoid movement.
    - Burns – Cool with water (not ice) and cover with a clean cloth.
    - Choking – Perform the Heimlich maneuver if needed.
    5.Perform CPR (if necessary) – If the person is unresponsive and breathing, start CPR.
    6. Prevent Shock – Keep the person warm and reassure them until help arrives.
    7. Do Not Move the Injured – Unless there is immediate danger, avoid moving them to prevent further injury.


  • Helping someone choking

    Steps to Help Someone Choking
    For Adults & Children (Above 1 Year)
    1. Assess the Situation – If they can cough or speak, encourage them to keep coughing.
    2. Call for Help – Dial 911 if the person cannot breathe, speak, or is turning blue.
    3. Perform the Heimlich Maneuver:
    - Stand behind the person and wrap your arms around their waist.
    - Make a fist and place it above their navel.
    - Grab your fist with the other hand and deliver quick, upward thrusts.
    - Repeat until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious.
    For Infants (Below 1 Year)
    1. Lay the baby face-down on your forearm, supporting their head.
    2. Give 5 back blows – Use the heel of your hand to deliver firm blows between the shoulder blades.
    3. Turn the baby over and give 5 chest thrusts – Use two fingers to press the chest.
    4. Repeat until the object is removed or the baby becomes unresponsive.
    If the Person Becomes Unconscious
    - Start CPR – Perform chest compressions and rescue breaths until help arrives.
    The Heimlich maneuver can dislodge objects blocking the airway in choking emergencies.

  • Recognizing a stroke

    Steps to Recognize and Respond to a Stroke
    Use the FAST Method:
    - Face – Ask the person to smile. Does one side droop?
    - Arms – Ask them to raise both arms. Does one drift downward?
    - Speech – Ask them to repeat a simple sentence. Is their speech slurred?
    - Time – If you notice any symptoms, call 911 immediately.
    2. Keep the Person Calm – Help them sit or lie down comfortably.
    3. Do Not Give Food or Drink – They may have difficulty swallowing.
    4. Monitor Their Condition – Note the time symptoms started and watch for worsening signs.
    5. Perform CPR (if necessary) – If they become unconscious and stop breathing, start CPR.

DOs and DON'Ts in an Emergency

  • DO: Stay calm and assess the situation.
  • DO: Call emergency services immediately.
  • DO: Provide first aid only if trained.
  • DO: Comfort the injured person and keep them conscious if possible.
  • DON'T: Move an injured person unnecessarily unless there's an immediate danger.
  • DON'T: Ignore safety protocols.
  • DON'T: Delay calling for professional help.
